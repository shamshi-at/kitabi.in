Questions, answered

Frequently asked questions

What is Kitabi?

Kitabi is a personal library app for readers who own books. Catalogue your shelf by scanning barcodes, keep a lending ledger of who borrowed what, log your reading with a timer, and see your year in pages. It works offline and syncs across your devices.

Is Kitabi free?

Yes. Kitabi is free, including the lending ledger that other apps put behind a paywall. There are no ads and your library is never sold or shared.

Can I import my Goodreads library into Kitabi?

Yes. Export your library from Goodreads as a CSV and import it in one tap — shelves, ratings, reviews and reading dates arrive mapped, not dumped. Any spreadsheet CSV works too, and you can export your whole library back out at any time.

Does Kitabi work offline?

Yes. Your library lives on your phone, so browsing, adding books and logging reading all work with no internet. Changes sync quietly when you’re back online.

Which languages does Kitabi support?

Books in any language. Kitabi grew up on Malayalam, Tamil and English bookshelves — titles display correctly in their own script, search understands transliteration (typing “Kayary” finds കയർ), and translations of the same work stay linked to each other.

Is my library public?

No — Kitabi is private by default. Every visibility switch starts off. You choose what to share: a single review, your shelf, or nothing at all.
