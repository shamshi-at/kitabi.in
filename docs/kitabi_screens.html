Cover treatments. Every cover — generated or real — sits in the same frame: rounded corners, left spine shade, drop shadow. Area 4

It runs both directions: what you’ve lent out, and what you’re borrowing — self-logged, or linked automatically when the friend who lent it to you is a Kitabi user too.

Area 8

Translations — one book, two works

The locked decision (5 Jul 2026): a translation is its own Work — its own title, authors, editions and its own rating pool — linked to the original by a shared translation_group_id. What was missing was the way in. These screens add it, and they start from the honest premise: the book in your hand is the translation. You add that; the original is context you attach to it, never a second thing you have to own. One field on the add form — Translated from — carries both cases: the original is already in the catalogue (pick it), or it isn't (add a four-field stub without leaving the sheet).

Area 9

Making things — every creation flow

“Add a book” means four different things, and the app currently funnels almost all of them through one form. This area maps every path that writes a row, then draws the screens that had no mockup. The through-line: you are always making one of four objects — a Work (a book that isn’t in the catalogue), an Edition (a printing of one that is), a Translation (its own Work, linked), or a shelf copy (yours, private, offline). Everything else — authors, publishers, series, genres — is created in passing, never as an errand of its own.

M11 · The genre sheet The same typeahead as the author, publisher and original pickers — one interaction, learned once. It exists to apply duplicate pressure: the book count next to each match is what stops "Sci-fi", "SciFi" and "Science fiction" from becoming three permanent rows that fragment everyone’s library filter. Genres get no case-folding server-side today (Type does), so this screen is the dedupe.
