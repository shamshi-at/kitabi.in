What I’d pick, and why

Direction B. It reads calmest, it matches the app’s “quiet, unhurried” voice, and it avoids the one thing this codebase has already been burned by — a floating layer that draws on top of modal sheets. A centerDocked FAB was tried and reverted on 9 Jul for exactly that reason (it punched through the lend sheet), and Direction A reintroduces a bottom-anchored floating bar into the same screen that opens sheets for status, shelves and lending.

Direction B’s real cost is that the actions scroll away. That’s worth accepting here: this page is usually short for an unowned book, and the reader who has scrolled to the buy links has already decided something. If it turns out to matter, B can grow A’s pinned bar later as a scroll-triggered addition — the reverse is a rewrite.

Open questions for you

  • Should “Wishlist” be equal-weight with “Add to library”? Mocked as secondary (outlined, slate — the wishlist colour). It could be a plain text link if you want the primary action to dominate more.
  • Does a borrowed book count as “not in library”? Today an unowned book still shows its lending card, so a book a friend lent you has history without an entry. That card is kept in all three directions but isn’t drawn above.
  • After adding, does the page jump to the YOURS tab or stay where the reader was? Staying put is less jarring; jumping confirms the action landed.
